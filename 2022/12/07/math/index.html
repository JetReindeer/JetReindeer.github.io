置换矩阵

Why Permutation matrix’s transpose equals to its inverse?

first . Permutation matrix is a square only including 1 and 0,and every row and column only have one ‘1’.

cause,when it transposes,and then do the multiplication , the ‘one times one’stuation only happens in the diagonal position. so the result will be an identity matrix.
thus, the transpose equals to inverse.

vector space

closure

if a bunch of vectors from the collection do vector additon as well as multiplication , it still in that collection , then we call that collection a vector space.

subspace

a subspace must contain the zero vector ,as the definition goes,after multiplication and addition , the result still exists in the space, then any vectors times by 0 , it gives the zero vector. So, importantly, the subspace must pass through the origin.

and a zero vector itself can be a subspace.

column space

The column space of a matrix A is the vector space made up of all linear combinations of the columns of A.
and always remember the origin is contained.

sovling Ax = b in the term of column space

The system of linear equations Ax = b is solvable exactly when b is a vector
in the column space of A

null space

The collection of solutions to Ax=0
